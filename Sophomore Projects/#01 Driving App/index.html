Driving
The Ultimate Beginner Guide
Basic Controls
Steering wheel - Used to control direction. Keep hands on wheel and eyes forward.
The right pedal is the accelerator which increases peed. The left is the brake which slows/stops the vehicle when pressed. Apply gently.
Gearshift/Stick - Shifts transmission between gears like "Park", "Drive", "Reverse".
Other - wipers, lights, defroster, horn, speedometer, turn signals, mirrors, clutch (manual)
Basic Signs
Stop Sign - means you must come to a complete stop at the sign or white stop line. You must wait until it's clear to proceed before moving forward.


Speed limit - indicate the maximum speed allowed by law in that area. You should not exceed this limit. Common ones are 25, 35, 45, 55 mph.


Yield - means you must give right of way to any crossing traffic and slow down to let it pass before proceeding. Only go if it's clear.


Traffic Lights - have signals for stop (red), proceed with caution (yellow), and go (green). Red means come to a complete stop. Yellow means clear the intersection or stop if not safe to proceed. Green means you may go if the intersection is clear. Obey the signals to avoid accidents.
Tips
Always adjust the seat so you can control the vehicle comfortably

Adjust your mirrors so you can see nearby cars without any blind spots

Always adjust your speed based on road and weather conditions.

Use headlights, AC, heater, etc when appropriate.
The most important thing is to stay focused on the road and your surroundings. Minimize distractions like phones, passengers, etc. Stay alert and don't zone out.  

Constantly check your mirrors. Watch your speed.

Try to keep your vehicle centered in your lane at all times.

Drive Defensively. Don't assume other drivers will do the right thing. Be cautious and leave enough space in case someone brakes suddenly or changes lanes abruptly.
How to Get a License
(In Louisiana)
Choose your License
#1 Be 15+

#2 Complete driver's education course

#3 Visit OMV with parent/guardian and documents (birth certificate, etc.)

#4 Pass vision and knowledge tests

#5 Pay fee

#6 Obtain permit allowing supervised practice driving
#1 Be 17+

#2 Complete driver's education or pre-licensing course

#3 Visit any OMV office with ID and completion certificate

#4 Pass vision, knowledge and road tests

#5 Pay fee

#6 Obtain unrestricted license allowing full driving privileges. Need to renew every 6 years.

(Note: If you already have the intermediate permit, you just have to visit your local OMV office at age 17+, give ID, and
pay a fee.)
#1 Be 16-17

#2 Hold learner's permit for 180 days

#3 Complete 50+ hours supervised practice driving

#4 Return to OMV with parent to document hours

#5 Pass road skills test

#6 Pay fee

#7 Obtain license with a few restrictions like nighttime limits
